who is Jane Goodall?

Jane Goodall ; her full name Valerie Jane Morris-Goodall was born on April 3, 1934 in London England. She was intrested in animal behavior when she was really young, and left school when she was 18.
She worked as a secratary and a film maker asisstant until she had enough money to go to Africa. When she got there she began helping paleontologist(the study of fossils and bones)
and antropologist(the study of how humans live) Louis Leakey. Her asociation with Louis eventually led her to starting of a camp in the Gombe Stream Game Reserve (which is now a national park)
so that she could look and observe the behavior of chimpanzees in the region.

what did she learn?

Through her research, Jane Goodall was able to correct a number of misunderstandings about chimpanzees. She found that the animals are omnivorous,
not vegetarian, they can make and use tools; and that they have social behaviours.

fun facts

Jane observed animals from a young age. Once, she sat for five hours in her family's chicken coop to watch a hen lay an egg. When she finally came out,she discovered that her family was really worried and had called the police to report her missing.
Jane Goodall was first introduced to chimpanzees when she was a baby. Her dad gave her a toy chimpanzee when she was one year old.she named it Jubilee and for years the toy remained with her. Even now,she keeps Jubilee on her dresser.
